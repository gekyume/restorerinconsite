Timeline of Rincón

Learn about Rincón's history, its rise to world recognition, its struggles with climate change and overdevelopment, and the ongoing efforts to protect the town.

1771

Rincón was founded by Luis de Añasco, a  resident from local San Germán.

1890

The famous Punta Higuero Lighthouse was built by the Spainards. This lighthouse is still in use today!

1968

The World Surf Competition was hosted in Rincón, and town became known worldwide.

2000

Two Californian  millionaires, Leon Richter and Dr. Chad Nielsen, contract a professor at the University of Southern California to determine the financial potential of Rincón. Richter and Nielsen travel to the town to begin developing propeerties, including on the Tres Palmes Reserve. By 2002,  "A Preliminary Study of the Value of Coastal Tourism in Rincón" was released, and there were six hotels by the end of the year.  

September 20, 2017

Hurricane Maria hits Puerto Rico, destroying 400 homes and bringing 4 feet of flooding to Rincón. Value of real estate in Puerto Rico drops considerably as the whole island begins to rebuild.

2019

To spur economic development, the governor of Puerto Rico signs into law the "Incentives Code of Puerto Rico", offering significant tax incentives for U.S residents to move and develop on the island. By 2020, it was estimated that there were 70-80 hotels in Rincón, along with around 1000 Airbnbs.

Sol Y Playa, a condominium complex in Rincón, begins developing an infinity pool on the Los Almendros beach. On July 3rd, an image of a giant hawksbill turtle trapped in the construction goes viral.

Protestors flock to the beach, demanding the construction be stopped. The government responds by sending hundreds of soldiers to protect the development. Camp Carey, an environmental activist group,  is revived, and sets up camp outside of Sol Y Playa.

On July 30, four congressional democrats write a letter to the U.S Fish and Wildlife Service asking for Los Almendros beach to be designated as a critical habitat for hawksbill and leatherback  turtles.

A few weeks later, a lower Puerto Rican court finds the Sol Y Playa development illegal, and orders its immediate destruction.


July 2021

Now

Efforts are in place to continue protecting the Rincón coastline from the threats of climate change and overdevelopment. The government has begun redemarcating coastlines to ensure that the erosion of the beaches is taken into account. Camp Carey and other activist groups continue to protest new developments in Rincón and around Puerto Rico.
