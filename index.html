Background

July 2021

It All Started With a Turtle

In July of 2021, a turtle was found trapped inside the construction of the Sol Y Playa infinity pool, an illegal development placed few meters from the ocean.

But this wasn't any ordinary turtle. The hawksbill turtle has been listed as Critically Endangered by the IUCN since 1970. And Rincón's Los Almendros Beach is one of the last places in Puerto Rico these turtles can safely live.

The people of Rincón acted. Local residents flocked to the beaches to protest. Camp Carey was revived. And the world listened.

A few months later, a PR court ruled that the structure was illegally built, and ordered its immediate destruction.

This was a huge win for the preservation of Puerto Rico's beaches from overdevelopment. But the work does not stop here. Join us to help protect and restore Rincón.

FROM  OVERDEVELOPMENT AND CLIMATE CHANGE

Our beaches are threatened, from the land and the sea. Overdevelopment in Rincón leads to less beach for our people and our species to thrive. Climate change has caused rising sea levels and powerful hurricanes, further eroding and narrowing our coastline.

The Solution

Join The Fight!

Whether you are a Rincón resident or a part of the global community, there are plenty of ways to help. Help organize protests, raise awareness, fundraise, and advocate for government action.

There are already several projects underway, including efforts to redermacate Rincón's beaches (to take into account the changes in coastline due to climate change), seek federal protection for hawksbill and leatherback turtle habitat, and to prosecute illegal developments near threatened beaches.
